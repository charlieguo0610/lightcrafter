What is Video Relighting?

Given an input video and a target illumination, re-render the whole video with consistent shadows, reflections, and materials.

Input Video
→
Relit Video

Why is it Hard?

It demands both long-form temporal consistency and a physically grounded model of light transport. Prior work follows two paradigms.

1 · Inverse + Forward Rendering

Reconstruct materials, geometry & lighting, then re-render (PBR or neural).

✗ Noisy reconstructions

✗ Misses global illumination

2 · Generative Video-to-Video

Condition a diffusion model on the relighting target (env map or text).

✗ Limited control & temporal drift

✗ Needs scarce paired data

Our Key Insight

Don't translate the input video. Translate a PBR proxy rendered under the target lighting.

✓ Intricate lighting control

The target is "baked" into the proxy render.

✓ Long-form consistency

The PBR proxy is temporally stable by construction.

✓ No env-map training

The model never has to learn illumination concepts.

How It Works

Recover a relightable scene state → render a frame-aligned PBR proxy under the target → a video diffusion refiner produces the photorealistic result.

Proxy First, Then Refine

The PBR proxy captures most of the scene–light interaction; the diffusion refiner adds the hard effects.
